# Prioritization Frameworks

## When to use this
When you have more ideas than capacity and need a defensible way to choose.

## Common frameworks
- **Value vs Effort** — a 2x2 grid. Do high-value/low-effort first; question high-effort/low-value.
- **RICE** — score by Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort. Good when you need to compare many items numerically.
- **Opportunity cost** — every "yes" is a "no" to something else. Name what you're deprioritizing.

## Core idea
Frameworks don't make the decision — they make the reasoning explicit and shareable, so the team aligns on WHY, not just WHAT.

## Common trap
Treating framework scores as objective truth. The inputs (impact, confidence) are estimates. Use them to structure discussion, not to end it.
