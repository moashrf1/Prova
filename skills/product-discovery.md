# Product Discovery

## When to use this
Before committing engineering time to a feature — to confirm the problem is real and worth solving.

## Core ideas
- **Fall in love with the problem, not the solution.** Validate the problem exists and matters before designing anything.
- **Talk to users** — short, open interviews about their actual behavior, not hypothetical preferences.
- **Look for evidence, not opinions** — what people do beats what they say they'd do.
- **Frame the problem** as a clear statement: who has it, when, and what it costs them today.

## Common trap
Asking leading questions ("Would you use a feature that does X?"). People say yes to be polite. Ask about past behavior instead ("When did you last face this? What did you do?").
